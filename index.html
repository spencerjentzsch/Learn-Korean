Writing Korean

Way to make it here! You ready to start learning Korean? The goal here is to teach you the basics of Korean writing in a couple hours. You won't be fluent but you will be able to read signs.

Intro

Although Korean looks like a character system, it is actually an alphabet of 14 basic consonants and 10 basic vowels. The letters are stacked together in syllable blocks that make them look more complex.

Take, for example the word 'hangul' (Korean writing).

Basic Vowels

아 (Ah)

Pronounced 'ah', like 'caw'.

야 (Ya)

Pronounced 'ya' like 'yadda yadda yadda'.

어 (Eoh)

Pronounced 'eoh' like 'song'.

여 (Yeo)

Pronounced 'yeoh' like 'yonder'.

오 (Oh with rounded lips)

Pronounced 'oh' with rounded lips. Like 'oak'.

요 (Yo with rounded lips)

Pronounced 'yo' with rounded lips, like 'yogi'.

우 (U with rounded lips)

Pronounced 'u' with rounded lips, like 'Uzbekistan'.

유 (Yu)

Pronounced 'yu' with rounded lips, like 'you'.

으 (uuuu)

Pronounced 'uuuu' like like being punched in the stomach.

이 (E)

Pronounced 'e', like 'meet'.

Basic Consonants

기역 (Soft K or G)

Pronounced like a soft 'k' or a 'g'.

니은 (N)

Pronounced like an 'n'.

디귿(Soft T or D)

Pronounced like a soft 't' or a 'd.

리을 (L or R)

Pronounced like an 'l' or an 'r'.

미음 (M)

Pronounced like an 'm'.

비읍 (Soft P or B)

Pronounced like a soft 'p' or a 'b'.

시옷 (S)

Pronounced like an 's'.

이응 (Silent or NG)

Pronounced like a silent placeholder at the beginning or an 'ng' at the end of a syllable.

자읒 (Soft CH or J)

Pronounced like a soft 'ch' or a 'j'.

치읓 (Hard CH)

Pronounced like a hard 'ch'. Lots of air.

키읔 (Hard K)

Pronounced like a hard 'k'. Lots of air.

티읕 (Hard T)

Pronounced like a hard 't'. Lots of air.

피읖 (Hard P)

Pronounced like a hard 'p'. Lots of air.

히읗 (H)

Pronounced like an 'h'.

Syllable Blocking

With the basics under your belt, it's time to start putting them together in syllable blocks.


Rule #1

Write the characters from top left to bottom right.

Rule #2

Every syllable will have at least one consonant and vowel.

Rule #3

A block always starts with a consonant.

Rule #4

The vowel determines the the shape of the stacking.
