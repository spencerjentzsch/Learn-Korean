Writing Korean

Hey! Welcome to Writing Korean. The goal here is to teach you the basics of Korean writing in a couple hours. You won't be fluent but you will be able to read signs. Let's do this.

  • Start Learning

Intro

Although Korean looks like a character system, it is actually an alphabet of 14 basic consonants and 10 basic vowels. The letters are stacked together in syllable blocks that make them look more complex.

Tips for Success

  • Don't romanize to another language!
  • Read and speak slowly

Basic Vowels

아 (Ah)

Pronounced like 'ah'.

야 (Ya)

Pronounced like 'ya'.

어 (Oh)

Pronounced like 'oh'.

여 (Yo)

Pronounced like 'yo'.

오 (Oh with rounded lips)

Pronounced like 'oh' with rounded lips.

요 (Yo with rounded lips)

Pronounced like 'yo' with rounded lips.

우 (U with rounded lips)

Pronounced like 'u' with rounded lips.

유 (Yu)

Pronounced like 'yu' with rounded lips.

으(uuuu)

Pronounced like 'u'.

이 (E)

Pronounced like 'e'.

Basic Consonants

기역 (K or G)

Pronounced like a 'k' or a 'g'.

니은 (N)

Pronounced like an 'n'.

디귿(T or D)

Pronounced like a 't' or a 'd.

리을 (L or R)

Pronounced like an 'l' or an 'r'.

미음 (M)

Pronounced like an 'm'.

비읍 (P or B)

Pronounced like a 'p' or a 'b'.

시옷 (S)

Pronounced like an 's'.

이응 (Silent or NG)

Pronounced like a silent placeholder or an 'ng' at the end of a syllable.

자읒 (CH or J)

Pronounced like a 'ch' or a 'j'.

치읓 (Hard CH)

Pronounced like a hard 'ch'. Lots of air.

키읔 (Hard K)

Pronounced like a hard 'k'. Lots of air.

티읕 (Hard T)

Pronounced like a hard 't'. Lots of air.

피읖 (Hard P)

Pronounced like a hard 'p'. Lots of air.

히읗 (H)

Pronounced like an 'h'.
